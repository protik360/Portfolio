§ 01. teaching approach

Teaching Philosophy

My approach to IELTS coaching is built on a single belief: templates get you 6.0, understanding gets you 7.5+.

Every student has a unique set of strengths and gaps. I identify yours in our first session and build a learning path around it — whether you need intensive writing feedback, speaking confidence, or listening strategy.

Lexical Resource

Moving beyond "good" and "bad" to precise, context-appropriate vocabulary.

Coherence & Cohesion

Structuring essays and responses that flow logically for the examiner.

Natural Pronunciation

Intonation, stress, and rhythm — not just accent reduction.

Examiner's Perspective

Teaching you to think like the person marking your paper.

"The goal is not to pass the IELTS exam. The goal is to use English so confidently that the IELTS score becomes a natural by-product."
— Pratik Poudel, on his teaching philosophy
